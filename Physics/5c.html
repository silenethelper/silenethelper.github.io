Why does the conductivity of the semiconductor increase with the rise of temperature?

Ans: When a semiconductor is heated, more and more electrons jump across the forbidden gap from valence band to conduction band where these are free to conduct electricity, Hence the conductivity increases with the increase in temperature.

Why does the width of depletion layer of a PN junction increases in reverse biasing?

Ans: During reverse biasing, the positive terminal of the external battery attracts electrons from the n-region and its negative terminal attracts holes from the p-region i.e. the majority charge carriers move away from the junction. This increases the width of the depletion layer.

What is an ideal junction diode?

Ans: A junction diode which conducts when forward biased and does not conduct when reverse biased is called an ideal junction diode. It offers zero resistance in forward biasing and infinite resistance in reverse biasing. 12. Why is zener diode fabricated by heavily doping both p and n side of the junction?

Ans: When zener diode is fabricated by heavily doping both p and n side of the junction the depletion region formed in zener diode is very thin and the electric field across the junction is very high even for a small reverse bias voltage.

What is an integrated circuit?

Ans: A miniature electronic circuit, consisting of many passive components like R and C and active components like diode and transistor, fabricated within a single semiconductor chip is called an integrated circuit.

Why is a NOT gate known as an inverter?

Ans: In the NOT gate as input is low (0), the output is high (1) and vice versa. So a NOT gate always inverts the input signal. Hence a NOT gate is known as an inverter.

How is a NOT gate different from AND or OR gate? Can it be made from junction diodes?

Ans: NOT gate has only one input while AND or OR gates can have two or more inputs. A NOT gate cannot be made from junction . It is realized by using a transistor.

Why is the semiconductor damaged by a strong current?

Ans: When a strong current is passed through a semiconductor, it heats up the semiconductor. Due to which the large number of covalent bonds break up in semiconductor, resulting large number of charge carriers. As a result of which, the material starts behaving as a conductor. At this stage, the semiconductor losses the roperty of low conduction, hence it is said to be damaged.

The resistance of PN junction is to when forward biased and is high when reversed biased. Explain.

Ans: A small increase in forward voltage across p-n junction shows large increase in forward current. Hence the resistance voltage/current) of p-n junction is low when forward biased. A large increase in reverse voltage across p-n junction shows small increase in reverse current. Hence the resistance of p-n junction is high when reverse biased.

A P type semiconductor has a large number of holes but still it is electrically neutral. Explain.

Ans: By doping Silicon (or Germanium) crystal with electrically neutral trivalent impurity a P type semiconductor is formed. After doping, the trivalent atom becomes negatively charged accepting an electron from the silicon crystal and an atom of the crystal becomes positively charged or a hole is created in a silicon atom. So if n trivalent atoms are electrons are accepted from the doped, n silicon crystal and n holes are created in the valance band of the crystal. Since the number of the trivalent ions which are negatives is equal to the number of holes in the P type crystal, it remains electrically neutral.
