Explain the significance of second law of thermodynamics.

Ans: The second law of thermodynamics simply mesures the direction of heat flow, i.e. heat always flows from a body at higher temperature to the body at lower temperature and heat never flows itself from a body at lower temperature. The second law of thermodynamics, according to; KelvinPlanck Statement: It is impossible to get continuous supply of work from a body which can transfer heat with a single source of heat. Clausius Statement: It is impossible to make heat flow from a body at a lower temperature to a body at a higher temperature without doing external work on the working substance.

Why does a refrigerator consume more power in summer than in winter to cool the same quantity of food by same degree?

Ans: A refrigerator is a heat engine which extracts heat from a cold body and rejects to hot body by doing work in working substance. Higher the temperature difference between hot and cold body, more energy is required to do work by the refrigerator. In summer, temperature difference is higher so that high energy is required to cool by the refrigerator in summer than winter. Hence, a refrigerator consumes more power in summer than in winter to cool the same quantity of food by same degree of temperature.

Why is conversion of heat into work not possible without a sink at lower temperature?

Ans: For converting heat energy into work continuously, a part of heat energy absorbed from the source has to be rejected. The heat energy can be rejected only if there is a body, whose temperature is less than that of the source. This body at lower temperature is the sink.

A gas expands adiabatically and reversibly, what is its change in entropy?

Ans: In an adiabatic process no heat enters or leaves the system. Hence dQ = 0 and there is no change in entropy in its reversible process, AS = 0. So, every reversible adiabatic process is a constant entropy process. That's why the reversible adiabatic process is also called a isentropic process. The increase in disorder resulting from the gas expansion is exactly balanced by the decrease in disorder associated with lower temperature and reduces molecular speed.

Why an engine cannot achieve 100 percent efficiency?

Ans: If Q1 is the amount of heat absorbed from the source and Q2 be the heat rejected to the sink, then the efficiency of the heat engine is given as Since Q1 > Q2, the efficiency, n is always less than unity. Hence the efficiency of heat engine cannot be 100%. For 100% efficiency n = 1, so that Q2 = 0 which is impossible according to the second law of thermodynamics. Therefore an engine cannot achieve 100% efficiency.

Can a room be cooled by leaving the doors of an electric refrigerator open in a closed room?

Ans: The room cannot be cooled by leaving the door of an electric refrigerator open. A refrigerator is a heat engine that operates in the reverse direction i.e. it extracts heat from a cold body and transfers it to a room which is at the higher temperature. Hence the temperature of room will not be cooled down but the room gets heated slightly.
