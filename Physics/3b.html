Does the sound of an explosion travel faster than the sound produced by a humming bee?

Ans: Both the sound of the explosion and the sound produced by a humming bee travel with the same speed. However, if the amplitude of the sound of the explosion is so large that the compressions and rarefactions result in appreciable change in temperature, then the speed of the sound of explosion will be greater than that produced by the humming bee.

The sound of a distant coming train can be easily detected by placing our ear near the rails. Why?

Ans. The speed of sound is quite large in solids as compared to that in gases. Further, due to high elasticity of solids, the sound waves do not die out soon as happens when sound travels in air. For these reasons, the sound of a distant coming train can be easily detected by placing ear near the rails.

When sound waves travel through medium, does the temperature at various points remain constant? Explain.

Ans: According to Laplace's correction for the velocity of sound, the process of compression and rarefaction occur so fast that neither heat is transferred nor it is taken from the surrounding means sound waves propagate through an adiabatic process.
