A restoring force is must for a body to execute S.H.M. Explain, why?

Ans: For a body to execute S.H.M., it should oscillate about its mean position. When the body is in the mean position, it possesses kinetic energy and by virtue of its motion the body moves from mean position to extreme position. The body can return to the mean position only if it is acted upon by a restoring force.

What are the characteristics of simple harmonic motion?

Ans: There are following characteristics of simple harmonic motion: (i) It is a periodic motion. (ii) The particle moves to and fro about the mean position in a straight line. (iii) The acceleration of the particle is directly proportional to the displacement from mean position, and its direction is opposite to the displacement from the mean position.

Why do we say that velocity and acceleration of a body executing S.H.M. are out of phase?

Ans: When a body is executing S.H.M., at the mean position acceleration is zero and at the extreme positions acceleration is maximum. On the other hand, the velocity is maximum at the mean position and zero at the extreme positions. Further, acceleration is always directed towards the mean position. Due to this reason, the velocity and acceleration of a body executing S.H.M. are out of phase.

What are the drawbacks of simple pendulum?

Ans: Following are the drawbacks of simple pendulum: (i) The requirements of an ideal simple pendulum cannot be realised in actual practice i.e. heavy point mass pendulum, weightless and inextensible string is not possible. (ii) The motion of the bob is not strictly linear. (iii) The resistance and buoyancy of air affects the motion of the bob which is supposed to be negligible. How will the period of oscillation change if the water begins to drain out of the hollow sphere from a fine hole at its bottom?

Ans: Time period, of oscillation of simple pendulum is T = 2101 Initially, when the sphere is completely filled with water, its C.G. lies at its centre. As water flows out, the C.G. begins to shift below the centre of the sphere. The effective length of the pendulum increases and hence its time period increases. When the sphere becomes more than half empty, its C.G. begins to rise up. The effective length of the pendulum decreases and time period T decreases. When the entire water is drained out of the sphere, the C.G. is once again shifted to centre of the sphere and the time period T attains its initial value.

A body is moving in a circular path with constant speed. Is this motion a simple harmonic? Why?

Ans: The motion of a body moving in a circular path is periodic. For simple harmonic motion, the motion should be both periodic and oscillatory, 1.e. the body in motion should come at rest momentarily at two extreme position during the motion. Hence the motion of body moving in a circular path with constant speed is periodic but not simple harmonic.
