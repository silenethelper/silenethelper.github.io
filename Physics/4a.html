The resistance of an ammeter must essentially be very small. Why?

Ans: An ammeter is a low resistance galvanometer. When it is connected in series in a circuit, the resistance of the circuit does not increase appreciably and consequently the current in the circuit remains unaffected. But if the resistance of the ammeter is high, the net resistance of the circuit becomes very high and the current in the circuit reduces and hence ammeter is not able to measure accurate current. Due to this reason, the resistance of an ammeter must essentially be very small.

Why is it essential that the resistance of a voltmeter be very high?

Ans: A voltmeter is a high resistance device. When it is connected in parallel across any component of a circuit, it draws a very small current from the main circuit. Most of the current passes through the element. Hence the p.d. across the component is not affected. If the resistance is small, the effective resistance is affected and most of the current passes through the voltmeter and the potential difference reduces across the component and the voltmeter cannot measure accurately. Due to this reason the resistance of a voltmeter be very high.

What happens when an ammeter is placed in parallel with a circuit?

Ans: When an ammeter is connected in parallel in a circuit, the resistance is considerably reduced. A large current flows through the circuit, which can damage the ammeter. Moreover, the ammeter will read the current passing through it only and not the current in the circuit.

What do you mean by shunt and what are its uses?

Ans: A small resistance connected in parallel to a galvanometer is called shunt. Shunt is used to a. convert a galvanometer into an ammeter. b. increase the current in a circuit. WC. increase the range of galvanometer.

Why do we prefer a potentiometer to measure emf of a cell rather than a voltmeter?

Ans: If the potentiometer is used, it doesn't draw any current from the cell and, hence, gives the actual value of e.m.f. of the cell whereas when a voltmeter is used to measure the e.m.f. of a cell, it draws some current from the cell and, hence, it gives a reading less than the actual value of e.m.f. of the cell. So, the-potentiometer acts as a voltmeter of infinite resistance. Therefore, the potentiometer measures the potential difference with less error than voltmeter. Due to this reason, we prefer potentiometer to measure the e.m.f. of a cell rather than a voltmeter.

Manganin wire is used instead of copper wire in potentiometer. Explain why.

Ans: The resistivity of manganin wire is high in comparison to copper wire. But temperature coefficient of manganin wire has low value as compared to copper wire. Hence, manganin wire provides high resistance with less change in its resistance due to temperature. Due to this reasons it is used in the potentiometer wire.

Why do we prefer potentiometer of longer length for accurate measurement?

Ans: The longer is the length of the potentiometer wire, the higher is the accuracy because the potential drop on the wire per unit length decreases as the length of the wire increases. Hence the potentiometer measures emf more accurately.

Wheatstone bridge method determination of resistance is considered to be more accurate than the voltmeter-ammeter method. Why?

Ans: Wheatstone bridge method is a null deflection method. The resistance determined does not depend on the deflection of the galvanometer and the supply voltage fluctuations. So, it is more accurate than other methods.
