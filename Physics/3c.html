An open organ pipe and a closed organ pipe have the same length and the same diameter. Do they produce the same quality of sound? Explain.

Ans: No. The quality of a sound is independent of the dimensions of the vibrating body and depends upon the number of harmonics (or overtones) present in it. In the open pipe, all harmonics are present. In the vibration of the air column in a closed pipe, only odd harmonics are present. In other words, the even harmonics (or the odd overtones) are missing in the closed pipe. Since they do not have the same number of harmonics, the quality of sound produced by open pipe differs from that produced by the closed pipe. Hence, sound produced by the open pipe has better quality than the sound produced by the closed pipe.

Is the wave speed the same as the speed of any part of the string for transv waves? Explain

Ans: There are two types of speed in the string i.e. wave speed and particle velocity. Wave speed is same in all part of string but the particle velocity varies from zero to some maximum value i.e, zero at node and maximum at anti-node. Hence, wave speed is not same as the speed of any part of the string for transverse waves.
