Beta particles penetrate through matter easily than that of alpha particle of the same energy. Why?

Ans: The velocity of B-particles is higher than a-particles. Due to very high velocity, B-particles spend a very short time close to the atoms of medium through which they pass. Therefore, B-particles transfer much less energy to the atoms and hence have the less possibility of ionization. In this case, B-particles loose much less energy. Due to this reason, B-particles penetrate through a matter easily than that of a-particle of the same energy.

Why do o-rays have the highest ionizing power?

Ans: a-particles have the highest ionizing power due to following two reasons: (i) It is the heaviest particle of all the three radioactive radiations. Therefore, it possesses greatest momentum and hence can knock out an orbital electron easily. (ii) It is most heavily charged. Define roentgen, red and rem.

Ans: The roentgen is the unit of radiation exposure equal to that quantity of X or gamma radiation that, in dry air, produces a total ionisation of 0.333 nC per 1.293 x 10-6 kg of air. The red is a unit equal to the absorbed dose of any radiation that release 100 erg (10-5 J) of energy per gram of absorbing material. The rem is the roentgen equivalent unit for man. The rem dose is found by multiplying the dose in rads by the relative biological effectiveness (REB).

The ionizing power of B-particles is less compared to a-particles, but their penetrating is more, why?

Ans: B-particles, being emitted with very high speed compared to a-particles, spend very small time near the atoms of the medium. So the probability of the atoms being ionized is comparatively less. But because of the same reason, their loss of energy is very slow and they are capable of penetrating through the medium up to a much high larger distance.

What is the physical significance of halflife of a radioactive substance?

Ans: The half life of a radioactive substance is the time after which half the original amount of substance gets decayed. For example, the half-life of 6C14 is about 5730 years. If at any instant, a piece of wood contains, say 1.0 x 1022 6C14 nuclei, then after a period of 5730 years, it will contain only 0.5 x 1022 nuclei.

Radioactivity is a spontaneous phenomenon. Explain.

Ans: In a given radioactive sample, the life of every atom is different. It is impossible to predict, which atom will decay first which afterwards. The phenomenon of radioactive decay is purely a chance process. In other words, the decay of a particular radioactive atom is known only, when it actually decays. This is why radioactivity is called a spontaneous phenomenon.

Explain, why natural radioactive nuclei are the nuclei of high mass number?

Ans: The B.E. per nucleon of the nuclei of high mass number is small as compared to that of the stable nuclei. Also, neutron to proton ratio of the nuclei of high mass number is different from that of stable nuclei. Such nuclei spontaneously decay to attain higher value of B.E. per nucleon and also to acquire appropriate neutron to proton ratio.

Why all radio-active elements are ultimately converted into lead?

Ans: All those elements which are heavier than lead are radioactive. This is because in the nuclei of heavy atoms, besides the nuclear attractive forces, repulsive forces between the protons are also effective and these forces reduce the stability of the nucleus. Hence, the nuclei of heavier elements are being converted into lighter and lighter elements by emission of radioactive radiation. When they are converted into lead, the emission is stopped because the nucleus of lead is stable. Why is its halflife so long?

Ans: The average energy of an aparticle formed within a nucleus is a few MeV, while the potential barrier around the 92U238 nucleus is about 25 MeV. Hence the half-life of 92U238 into 90Th234 is long.
