Explain why two flash lights held closed together will not produce an interference pattern on a distance screen?

Ans: An interference pattern is obtained only when the two flash lights is coherent. Two sources of light are said to be coherent if they emit continuous light waves of the same frequency, same wavelength and have zero phase difference or constant phase difference at all times. But it is not possible for two independent sources to be coherent. Hence two flash lights held closed together will not produce an interference pattern on a distant screen.

Can two independent sources of light produce interference?

Ans: No, the two independent sources of light cannot produce sustained interference. For producing interference pattern, we need two coherent sources vibrating in the same phase, or at a constant phase difference. The two independent sources are not coherent hence they can not produce interference.

Why the two sources of light to be close to each other for the production of good interference pattern?

Ans: For the production of good interference pattern, two sources of light must be close to each other. Because, the fringe width in the interference pattern is inversely proportional to the separation between the coherent sources (ß = 2D/d). When the distance d between the coherent sources is decreased, the fringe width goes on increasing and the clear interference pattern can be observed on the screen. Hence the two sources of light must be close to each other.

When two coherent sources are far apart, interference pattern cannot be detected. Why?

Ans: We know that the fringe width B is inversely proportional to the separation between the sources i.e. B a 1/d. Therefore, when d is increased, the fringe width B goes on decreasing and at very large distance, it becomes undetectable.

Does the interference of light waves obey the law of conservation of energy? Explain.

Ans: For a single source of light, the distribution of energy is uniform. But, for two coherent sources, they produce non-uniform distribution of energy and varies from maximum intensity to minimum intensity. At a point of maximum intensity, the energy is 4 times that from a single source whereas at a point of minimum intensity, the energy is zero. However, the average energy is same as if two sources acted separately without interference. Indeed, the energy which apparently disappears at the minimum point is still present at the maximum point. Therefore, the interference phenomenon obeys the law of conservation of energy.
